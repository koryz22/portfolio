// testimony

"My name is Kory and today I'll be sharing my testimony, what God has done throughout my life, and how He has led me to faith.

Church has always been a part of my life. From attending Sunday Service with my family, to going on youth retreats, I was actively involved within the community.

However, this attendance was the extent of my faith. As a kid, I could merely name a few books in the Bible or explain what the gospel was. I often questioned my beliefs and why I went to church. But due to a lack of interest, I pushed these doubts aside. Christianity to me, was more about community.

During highschool, my faith grew even weaker as I started to focus on my future and how to be successful. I thought I had it all figured out: get into a good college, get a good job, make money, and enjoy life. However, this self-reliant mindset became one I was familiar with, and I lost sight of what god I achieved, I was never satisfied.

And so, I realized I wasn't satisfied. I found myself in a constant cycle, chasing the feeling of emptiness, and searching for meaning in the world. I started to party, go to raves, and prioritize materialism. Over time, my heart had hardened and completely turned away from the Lord. Fueled by selfishness and lust, I became blinded to the truth.

As an unsuspecting result, I felt even more so out of touch with deep depression and anxiety. By the grace of God, the rest of my life is the lowest and became my source of strength and comfort. I found myself at Beacon Community Church in Irvine, seeking the love and wisdom of the Lord. And it was there that I encountered the truth — that God was not simply a distant figure, but rather a personal God: one who loved me deeply, one who sent His only Son to die for sinners who lifted me up and brought me to who I am today. I found myself in the Lord's embrace.

The truth is this, God, our good and loving ruler, created us and the world. He has given us all that we need for this life and for the next. However, we began to live our own lives and rightly deserve eternal death. But, in love, God sent His Son, Jesus Christ, to die on the cross, taking the punishment we deserved, so that through faith in Him we might have eternal life. In our place, he suffered greatly and took our judgement upon himself by dying on a cross. But on the third day, God raised Jesus from death, and by his grace, granting us the decision to either continue our rebellion, or to trust in Christ alone for the forgiveness of our sins.

This truth, the gospel, didn't become clear to me until I learned the forgiveness of God. While I was separated from God by my sin, I was also subject to the wrath of God. But He is merciful and offers atonement through the sacrifice of His own Son. And this truth brings me more hope and peace compared to the temporary things I was gaining in the world.

If you are a non-believer in this room today, I encourage you to take a step of faith and explore this truth for yourself. For a long time, I lived my life like I didn't need God, and was constantly chasing the desires of my heart. Matthew 6:33 says, Jesus said in His Sermon on the Mount 'But seek first the kingdom of God and his righteousness, and all these things will be added to you.'

It is only because of God, that we can have and experience anything. So I exhort myself for the rest of my life, to be self-less, and exhort my joy in depletion. As I strive to live with Christ as my Lord and Savior, I humbly submit my testimony to be a reminder of the love of God. Thank you."
